# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Approved community partner organisation responders — coordinators at SG Cares Volunteer Centres, Silver Generation Office outreach teams, eldercare providers, pharmacy partners, and transport partners. They use this dashboard during active public health emergency operations (COVID-19, Dengue, Haze). Context is time-pressured: they need to triage incoming caregiver requests, assign teams, and track fulfilment without friction. They are not data analysts — they are coordinators doing operational work.

## Product Purpose

ORCA Community Dashboard is a case management and community response operations platform. It converts individual caregiver support requests (medication collection, welfare checks, transport, food delivery, advisory) into coordinated community response. Success means a responder can open the dashboard, understand the situation at a glance, take action on the highest-priority requests, and hand off — all within minutes. It is not an analytics product; it is an action product.

## Brand Personality

Urgent, focused, operational. The interface should feel like a well-designed operations room: every element earns its place, information hierarchy is unambiguous, and actions are never more than one click away. It should feel like the people behind it take the work seriously.

## Anti-references

- Consumer apps: no Notion/Linear playfulness, no startup-y rounded softness, no gamified micro-interactions
- Government portals: no dense cluttered layouts, no legacy e-gov typography, no bureaucratic grey

## Design Principles

1. **Action over analysis** — surfaces exist to enable decisions, not to display data for its own sake. Every component should answer "what do I do next?"
2. **Hierarchy earns attention** — urgency and status must be legible at a glance. High-priority items should feel visually heavier without resorting to alarm aesthetics.
3. **Operational clarity** — labels, status names, and workflow steps use the exact vocabulary responders use in the field. No UX euphemisms.
4. **Calm under pressure** — the palette and spacing should reduce cognitive load, not amplify it. Restraint in decoration; confidence in structure.
5. **Respect the context** — this is used during emergencies. Every interaction must be fast, predictable, and forgiving of mistakes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Colour is never the sole signal for urgency or status — always paired with a label or icon. Keyboard navigable. Designed primarily for desktop use by responders at workstations.
